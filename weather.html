Rain likelihood: ~10% chance on any given day in early September. Any rain is typically light and short-lived — passing showers, not all-day events. The west side (Kāʻanapali, Lāhainā) is the driest part of the island.

What This Means for Packing

Daytime: Light clothing, swimwear, sandals. It's warm — you'll live in shorts.

Evenings: A light layer (hoodie or long-sleeve) for trade winds and sunset on the beach.

Rain gear: A packable rain jacket just in case — but you probably won't use it.

Sun protection: Reef-safe sunscreen, hat, sunglasses. The UV index is no joke.

Haleakalā: If you're doing sunrise at the summit, bring actual warm clothes — it's ~40°F at 10,000 ft before dawn. Pants, jacket, beanie.

Microclimates

Maui has multiple climate zones on one island. Where we'll be:

Kāʻanapali / LāhaināSunny, dry, warm — this is where Honua Kai is. The leeward (west) side gets the least rain.
Road to Hana (east side)Lush, tropical — expect passing showers and waterfalls. That's why it's so green.
Haleakalā summitAlpine desert. ~40°F at sunrise, can be windy. Then 80°F at the beach two hours later.
Upcountry (Makawao, Kula)Cooler — mid-70s. Worth a visit for the views and farm stands.
