Ideas for Maui — add yours

On the List

🤿

Scuba Diving

Shore diving at Makena Landing and Honolua Bay — no boat needed. Turtles, reef fish, beginner-friendly.

Michael
⛵

Beach Catamaran

Rent a small catamaran — take turns going out. Easy to learn, no license needed for small boats.

Michael
🛣️

Road to Hana

Full day trip. Waterfalls, black sand beach at Waiʻānapanapa (separate state park reservation required — gostateparks.hawaii.gov), banana bread stands, 600+ curves. Leave early — motion sickness meds help on the winding roads.

🌄

Haleakalā Sunrise

10,023 ft summit. Dress warm — ~40°F at the top. Requires: sunrise reservation ($1/car at recreation.gov, 60 days ahead) + national park entry pass ($30/car).

Also Worth Considering

Snorkeling

Honolua Bay, Kapalua Bay, Black Rock. Check daily snorkel report each morning.

Lūʻau

Old Lāhainā Lūʻau is the classic. ~$230/person. Book immediately — Labor Day weekend sells out months ahead.

Surf Lessons

Lāhainā has gentle breaks for beginners. Group lessons ~$85–125/person.

ʻĪao Valley

Easy paved hike, lush tropical valley, iconic 1,200-ft ʻĪao Needle peak. Good for all ages — stroller-friendly path.

Beach Day

Kāʻanapali Beach right at the resort. Walk the boardwalk to Whalers Village for shopping, shave ice, and sunset cocktails at Duke's.

Farmers Market

Napili Farmers Market (Wed & Sat mornings) and Maui Swap Meet (Sat). Fresh pineapple, local honey, handmade crafts.
